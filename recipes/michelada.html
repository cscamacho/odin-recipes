Michelada Recipe

A michelada is a Mexican drink that has numerous variations throughout Mexico. In the 2010s, major U.S. beer producers began marketing cervezas preparadas, illustrating the wide variety of recipes and acknowledging its popularity.

Michelada's have been commercialized, but many Mexican's tend to prepare their own micheladas how they enjoy them and have learned from family.

It is very true that no michelada will taste the same. There is a variance in flavors as it is easy to adjust to your own liking. When making michelada's, be creative and make it taste how you want it!

Ingredients Include:

  • Beer (Commonly Modelo or Tecate)
  • Limes
  • Tajin
  • Hot Sauce
  • Clamato
  • Ice

Steps to Prepare

  1. Coat the rim of the glass with lime juice.
  2. Dip the rim into a tray of tajin, coating the rim.
  3. Add ice to the glass.
  4. Add hot sauce to sides of glass to the spice level of your choice.
  5. Cut up limes, juice them into the glass and keep in. Add to your desired acidic level.
  6. Pour beer in.
  7. Top off with clamato. If you want it to be more hoppy, add more beer. Less hoppy, add more clamato!
